Crochet Terms & Abbreviations

A quick reference guide for crochet shorthand and stitch names you’ll use in patterns.

Glossary

Working Yarn
The strand of yarn attached to the ball or skein — the one you crochet with.

Slip Knot
The starting knot that attaches yarn to your hook.

Stitch (ST)
One completed crochet movement (like a chain, SC, or DC).

Chain (CH)
The foundation stitch in crochet. Creates the starting row or adds space.

Yarn Over (YO)
Wrapping the yarn over the hook from back to front. Used in almost all stitches.

Slip Stitch (SL ST)
A short stitch used for joining or moving without adding height.

Single Crochet (SC)
A basic stitch that creates a dense, tight fabric.

Half Double Crochet (HDC)
Taller than SC, shorter than DC. Creates a medium fabric.

Double Crochet (DC)
A tall stitch that works up quickly and leaves an airy texture.

(19)
A number in parentheses tells you how many stitches should be in that row.
