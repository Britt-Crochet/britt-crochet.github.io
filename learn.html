Materials Needed

These are the basic items you need to crochet:

  1. Medium weight size 4 100% cotton yarn. Any yarn you have will do!
  2. Size H / 5mm crochet hook. Follow the guidelines on your yarn for hook size. I recommend starting with a larger hook until you learn your tension.
  3. Any scissors or shears will work
  4. Large sewing needles These work great
  5. Stitch markers

A version of all of these items can be found at your local craft store.

Slip Knot

A slip knot creates a secure, adjustable starting loop on your hook. It's the first step in most crochet projects because it anchors your yarn and allows you to begin your chaing without it unraveling.

Step 1: Cross the yarn
Take your yarn and make a cross, placing the working yarn (the yarn attached to the ball) underneath the end of yarn

Step 2: Pull working yarn through loop>
Step 3: Tighten the loop
Step 4: Insert hook and secure
Don't make the loop too tight on your hook. This can make it hard to work into.

Starting Chain

The starting chain is the foundation of most crochet projects. It creates a base row of chain stitches that you will build your first row of crochet into. Each chain stitch is abbreviated CH in crochet patterns. Starting chains help establish the width of your project and ensure your edges are even.

Step 1: Yarn Over
A yarn over (YO) is when you wrap the working yarn (the yarn attached to the ball) over the hook from back to front.
Step 2: Pull Through
Pull the working yarn through the loop on your hook.
Step 3: First Chain
The yarn is fully pulled through the loop, creating your first chain stitch.
Step 4: Chain 20
Continue making chains until you have 20 CH.

Single Crochet / Foundation Row

The single crochet (SC) stitch is the foundation of many crochet projects. It’s a short, dense stitch that creates a tight fabric. You’ll work into the chains of your foundation row and mark your first and last stitches with stitch markers to keep track of the edges.

Step 1: Insert Hook
Gently insert your hook into the 2nd chain from the hook. The skipped first chain helps give your row a clean edge.
Step 2: Yarn Over (YO)
Wrap the working yarn over your hook.
Step 3: Pull Through Loop
Pull the yarn through the chain stitch. You now have two loops on your hook.
Step 4: Yarn Over Again
Wrap the yarn over your hook once more, getting ready to finish the stitch.
Step 5: Complete Your Single Crochet
Pull the yarn through both loops on the hook. Congratulations! Your first SC stitch is done!
This is where the basic SC instructions end.
Step 6: Place Your Stitch Marker
Mark the first stitch of your row with a stitch marker to easily track where it begins.
Step 7: Foundation Row Complete
Your first row of SC is complet. There should be 19 stitches in total (remember, the skipped chain at the start doesn’t count).
Step 8: Chain 1
Make 1 chain at the end of the row. This raises your yarn for the next row..
Step 9: Turn Your Work
Flip your project to crochet back across the row.
Step 10: Hook Into First Stitch
Insert your hook into the first stitch of the previous row and yarn over, ready to continue your SC stitches.
Step 11: Add a Stitch Marker
Place a stitch marker in the first stitch of this row to track where it begins.
Step 12: Track the Last Stitch
At the end of the row, mark the last stitch to make it easy to find where the row ends.
Step 13: Move Marker for Next Row
Move the stitch marker to the first stitch of the next row. Your edge is now perfectly guided for smooth, even rows.
